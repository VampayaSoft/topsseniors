What is Montessori Method?

The Montessori Method with older adults with dementia has taken root. The Montessori philosophy is that each person deserves respect and dignity. We must see “the person” in a person with dementia. As Montessori revolutionized education for children by providing choice within prepared environments, the same way of thinking can revolutionize the way we work with persons with dementia.

By creating materials and environments that allow accomplishment and a sense of purpose, The Montessori Method applied to dementia emphasizes the use of remaining capabilities, the ability to improve with practice, and the need to enable a person who happens to have dementia to be as independent as possible, to engage in purposeful and meaningful activity.

Providing choice in every interaction with the person with dementia is a key feature of this approach, such as holding up two snacks and asking, “Would you like to eat this snack or this one?”

Having the opportunity to see dishes on a menu enables persons with dementia to make informed choices during meals and selection of entertainment. Activities focus on principles such as demonstrating how to perform an activity before asking a person with dementia to do it (using clear, slow demonstrations as well as the application of the three-period lesson), breaking down activities into steps that can be performed one at a time, extensive use of external aids and templates.

Services

  • Exercise classes to help improve mobility, endurance and function, through activities that promote improved posture, balance and motion.
  • Conditioning and activities to improve alertness and daily function.
  • Breathing exercises and activities to promote increased oxygen to the body and brain, improving alertness, endurance, sleep, mental and physical function.
